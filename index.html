About this App

This application was designed and developed by Esri's Applications Prototype Lab in Redlands, California. The application was built using the following technologies:

  • ArcGIS API for JavaScript by Esri
  • Bootstrap by Twitter Inc.
  • D3.js by Mike Bostock
  • jQuery by the jQuery Foundation Inc.

Data in this presentation is courtesy of Dr. Camilo Mora from the University of Hawai'i's Department of Geography. The following is an extract from Global risk of deadly heat anomalies (2015) by Mora et al.

Survey of published cases of heat-related mortality. We searched for peer-reviewed publications on heat-related mortality in Google Scholar, PubMed, and the Web-of-Science, limiting the search to papers published between 1980 and 2014 and using the following keywords: (human OR people) AND (mortality OR death OR lethal) AND (heat OR temperature). We searched for papers primarily in English but also included papers in Spanish, French, Japanese, and Chinese when found. We reviewed the titles and abstracts of the first 30,000 citations in Google Scholar and all citations from other databases and selected any publications on heat-related human mortality (we also searched for additional sources in the references). These efforts resulted in 911 papers from which we collected information on the place and dates of lethal heat events, as well as the percent excess mortality when reported.
